Filipino Cooking Recipes

Learn to cook Filipino dishes step by step!

Chicken Adobo

A classic Filipino dish with soy sauce, vinegar, and garlic.

Tip: Marinate the chicken for at least 30 mins for better flavor.

How to Cook:

  1. Marinate chicken with soy sauce, garlic, and pepper for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and sauté garlic until fragrant.
  3. Add chicken and sear until lightly browned.
  4. Pour in vinegar and let it boil without stirring for 3 minutes.
  5. Add water and bay leaves, then simmer for 30 minutes or until chicken is tender.
  6. Adjust taste and serve hot with rice.

Sinigang na Baboy

Sour soup with pork and vegetables, easy for beginners.

Tip: Add tamarind paste gradually to control sourness.

How to Cook:

  1. Boil pork in water with tomato and onion until tender.
  2. Add radish, sitaw (string beans), and eggplant, cook for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Add tamarind paste or sinigang mix to taste.
  4. Serve hot with rice.

Kare-Kare

A rich Filipino stew made with peanut sauce, vegetables, and tender beef.

Tip: Serve with bagoong (shrimp paste) for authentic Filipino flavor.

How to Cook:

  1. Boil beef until tender.
  2. Add eggplant, string beans, and banana blossom.
  3. Mix peanut butter and annatto powder into the broth.
  4. Simmer until thick and flavorful.
  5. Serve hot with bagoong and rice.

Bistek Tagalog

A savory Filipino beef steak marinated in soy sauce and calamansi juice.

Tip: Marinate the beef for at least 1 hour for a richer flavor.

How to Cook:

  1. Marinate beef in soy sauce and calamansi.
  2. Pan-fry beef until browned.
  3. Sauté onions until soft.
  4. Combine beef and onions with marinade.
  5. Simmer for 10 minutes and serve.

Bulalo

A comforting beef marrow soup with vegetables and corn.

Tip: Simmer the beef slowly to achieve a flavorful broth.

How to Cook:

  1. Boil beef shank until tender.
  2. Add onions and corn.
  3. Season with fish sauce and pepper.
  4. Add cabbage and green beans.
  5. Serve hot.

Pancit Canton

A popular Filipino noodle dish served during celebrations.

Tip: Avoid overcooking the noodles to keep them firm.

How to Cook:

  1. Sauté garlic, onions, and meat.
  2. Add vegetables and broth.
  3. Add pancit noodles.
  4. Mix until noodles absorb the broth.
  5. Serve warm.

Lumpiang Shanghai

Crispy Filipino spring rolls filled with seasoned meat.

Tip: Roll tightly to prevent the filling from spilling while frying.

How to Cook:

  1. Mix ground pork, carrots, onions, and seasonings.
  2. Wrap in lumpia wrappers.
  3. Roll tightly and seal edges.
  4. Deep fry until golden brown.
  5. Serve with sweet chili sauce.

Leche Flan

A creamy Filipino dessert topped with sweet caramel.

Tip: Strain the mixture before steaming for a smoother texture.

How to Cook:

  1. Prepare caramelized sugar in a llanera.
  2. Mix egg yolks, milk, and sugar.
  3. Pour into the llanera.
  4. Steam for 30–40 minutes.
  5. Cool before serving.

Chicken Tinola

A healthy chicken soup with ginger, papaya, and chili leaves.

Tip: Use fresh ginger for a more flavorful broth.

How to Cook:

  1. Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger.
  2. Add chicken and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Pour water and simmer until chicken is tender.
  4. Add green papaya and cook for 10 minutes.
  5. Add chili leaves and serve hot.

Pork Sisig

A sizzling Filipino favorite made with chopped pork and onions.

Tip: Add mayonnaise for a creamier sisig.

How to Cook:

  1. Boil pork until tender.
  2. Grill or fry until crispy.
  3. Chop into small pieces.
  4. Mix with onions, chili, soy sauce, and calamansi.
  5. Serve on a sizzling plate.

Laing

Dried taro leaves cooked in rich coconut milk.

Tip: Do not stir immediately after adding the dried leaves.

How to Cook:

  1. Boil coconut milk with garlic, onion, and ginger.
  2. Add dried taro leaves.
  3. Simmer until tender.
  4. Add chili peppers.
  5. Serve with rice.

Ginataang Kalabasa

Squash and string beans cooked in creamy coconut milk.

Tip: Add shrimp for extra flavor.

How to Cook:

  1. Sauté garlic and onions.
  2. Add squash and coconut milk.
  3. Cook until squash softens.
  4. Add string beans.
  5. Season and serve.

Pork Menudo

A tomato-based pork stew with vegetables.

Tip: Let it simmer longer for tender pork.

How to Cook:

  1. Sauté garlic, onion, and tomatoes.
  2. Add pork and cook.
  3. Pour tomato sauce and water.
  4. Add potatoes and carrots.
  5. Serve warm.

Chicken Afritada

Chicken cooked in tomato sauce with vegetables.

Tip: Add bell peppers at the end to keep them crisp.

How to Cook:

  1. Brown the chicken.
  2. Sauté garlic, onions, and tomatoes.
  3. Add tomato sauce and water.
  4. Add potatoes and carrots.
  5. Serve hot.

Beef Caldereta

A hearty beef stew with tomato sauce and liver spread.

Tip: Simmer the beef until very tender.

How to Cook:

  1. Brown the beef.
  2. Add garlic, onions, and tomato sauce.
  3. Simmer until beef is tender.
  4. Add vegetables and liver spread.
  5. Serve hot.

Arroz Caldo

A comforting Filipino rice porridge with chicken.

Tip: Top with boiled egg and fried garlic.

How to Cook:

  1. Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger.
  2. Add chicken pieces.
  3. Add rice and broth.
  4. Cook until the rice becomes soft.
  5. Serve hot.

Turon

Sweet banana spring rolls coated with caramelized sugar.

Tip: Use ripe saba bananas for the best taste.

How to Cook:

  1. Wrap banana and jackfruit in lumpia wrapper.
  2. Seal the wrapper.
  3. Fry until golden brown.
  4. Sprinkle sugar while frying.
  5. Serve warm.

Halo-Halo

A famous Filipino dessert with shaved ice, milk, fruits, and sweets.

Tip: Add ube ice cream for extra flavor.

How to Prepare:

  1. Place sweetened beans and fruits in a tall glass.
  2. Add shaved ice.
  3. Pour evaporated milk.
  4. Top with leche flan and ube ice cream.
  5. Mix well before eating.
